About The Conference

The Web Conference

The Web Conference (formerly known as International World Wide Web Conference, abbreviated as WWW) is a yearly international academic conference on the topic of the future direction of the World Wide Web. This conference has been the premier venue to present and discuss progress in research, development, standards, and applications of the topics related to the Web.

The conference brings together researchers, developers, users, and commercial ventures—indeed all who are concerned with the Web and its evolution. The conference provides a forum for discussion about the evolution of the Web, the standardization of its associated technologies, and the impact of those technologies on society and culture.

History

The first conference of this series was held in 1994 at CERN in Geneva, where the Web was born. Since then, the conference has been held in various locations around the world, typically during April or May.

The conference is organized by the International World Wide Web Conferences Steering Committee (IW3C2), which oversees the conference series and selects the location and local organizing committee for each conference.

Over the years, The Web Conference has become the premier venue for researchers, academics, businesses, and standard bodies to come together and discuss the latest advancements in Web technologies and their impact on society and culture.

The Web Conference 2026

The 2026 edition of The Web Conference will be held in Dubai, United Arab Emirates, from April 13-17, 2026. Dubai, with its cutting-edge infrastructure and position as a global hub for innovation and technology, provides an ideal setting for discussing the future of the Web.

The conference will feature a high-quality program including research sessions, posters and demonstrations, a PhD symposium for junior scholars, workshops, tutorials, a developers track for practitioners, as well as thought-provoking keynote speakers, panels, and special tracks.

Code of Conduct

The Web Conference is dedicated to providing a harassment-free conference experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, age, religion, or nationality.

We do not tolerate harassment of conference participants in any form. Sexual language and imagery is not appropriate for any conference venue, including talks. Conference participants violating these rules may be sanctioned or expelled from the conference without a refund at the discretion of the conference organizers.

For the full Code of Conduct and reporting procedures, please visit our Code of Conduct page.

ACM Ethics

The Web Conference 2026 adheres to the ACM Code of Ethics and ACM Policy Against Harassment. We are committed to the highest ethical standards in all aspects of the conference.
