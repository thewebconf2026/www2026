# Newsletter #1 - Welcome and Call for Contributions

## Welcome to the ACM Web Conference 2026

The 2026 edition of the ACM Web Conference will be held in Dubai, United Arab Emirates (UAE) from April 13 to April 17, 2026. Check our website (https://www2026.thewebconf.org/).

The conference organizing committee is preparing an edition rich in scientific exchanges. In addition to the usual research tracks, the conference will feature the following additional tracks and events: Short Papers, Industry, Workshops, Tutorials, Ph.D. Symposium, and Demos, as well as three special tracks:  Competitions (for the first time in the conference series), Web4All, and History of Web. As usual, the Web4Good conference will be co-located with the main conference.

## TheWebConf'2026 Main Research Tracks 

We are happy to announce that Call for Papers for the Main Research tracks 
(https://www2026.thewebconf.org/calls/research-tracks.html) has been published by our PC Chairs, Francesco Bonchi (CENTAI, Italy, and Eurecat, Spain), Ido Guy (Meta, Israel, and Ben-Gurion University of the Negev, Israel), and Emine Yilmaz (University College, UK and Amazon, UK).

## Other Events

The History of the Web (https://www2026.thewebconf.org/program/history-of-web.html) Special Track, chaired by Dame Wendy Hall and Oshani Seneviratne, is a by invitation-only event.

## Colocated Conference

We are delighted to share that, like in previous years, the Web4All (https://www.w4a.info/2026/) conference, the premier venue for web accessibility research, will be co-located with our conference.

## Nominations

Submit your nomination now for the Seoul Test of Time Award (https://www2026.thewebconf.org/calls/seoul-test-of-time-award.html), chaired by Marc Najork.
