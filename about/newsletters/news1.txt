# Newsletter #1 - Welcome and Call for Contributions

## Welcome to the ACM Web Conference 2026

The 2026 edition of the ACM Web Conference will be held in Dubai, United Arab Emirates (UAE) from April 13 to April 17, 2026. Check our website (https://www2026.thewebconf.org/).

The conference organizing committee is preparing an edition rich in scientific exchanges. In addition to the usual research tracks, the conference will feature the following additional tracks and events: Short Papers, Industry, Workshops, Tutorials, Ph.D. Symposium, and Demos, as well as three special tracks:  Competitions (for the first time in the conference series), Web4All, and History of Web. As usual, the Web4Good conference will be co-located with the main conference.

## TheWebConf'2026 Main Research Tracks 

We are happy to announce that Call for Papers for the Main Research tracks 
(https://www2026.thewebconf.org/calls/research-tracks.html) has been published by our PC Chairs, Francesco Bonchi (CENTAI, Italy, and Eurecat, Spain), Ido Guy (Meta, Israel, and Ben-Gurion University of the Negev, Israel), and Emine Yilmaz (University College, UK and Amazon, UK).

## Other Events

The History of the Web (https://www2026.thewebconf.org/program/history-of-web.html) Special Track, chaired by Dame Wendy Hall and Oshani Seneviratne, is a by invitation-only event.

## Colocated Conference

We are delighted to share that, like in previous years, the Web4All (https://www.w4a.info/2026/) conference, the premier venue for web accessibility research, will be co-located with our conference.

# Newsletter #2 - Venue Revealed and Important Updates

## Conference Venue
The conference venue has been confirmed as the InterContinental Dubai Festival City. The location is highly accessible and situated near the waterfront, with several hotels in close proximity. Additional details regarding the venue and accommodation options will be published on the conference website shortly.


## Visa Information
Obtaining a visa for the UAE is generally straightforward for most countries. Detailed and up-to-date information on UAE visa requirements will be made available on the conference website soon.


## ACM Open Access and APC Transition
The conference is transitioning to the ACM Open Access publishing model  
(see https://www2026.thewebconf.org/about/acm-policy.html). Under this model, authors are required to pay an Article Processing Charge (APC) for each published paper if none of the authorsâ€™ institutions are enrolled in the ACM Open program. The current APC is USD 250 for ACM members and USD 350 for non-ACM members. Papers for which the APC is not paid will not be included in the ACM Digital Library.


## Keynote Speakers
We are pleased to announce that two keynote speakers have been confirmed:  
Dr Mounia Lalmas (Head of Tech Research & Personalization at Spotify) and Prof Katrina Ligett (Professor of Computer Science and Director of the Federmann Center for the Study of Rationality at The Hebrew University of Jerusalem).


## Main Tracks Status
Submissions are now closed, and we are pleased to report a substantial increase in submissions compared to last year (+63% in Research Tracks, and +20% in the Industrial Track). The review process is nearing completion.
